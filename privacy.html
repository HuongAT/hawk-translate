Privacy Policy

What we do.
What we don't.

The short version: Hawk Translate doesn't collect, sell, or transmit your data to us. Below is the long version — written in plain language, not legalese.

Last updated: April 2026 · Effective immediately
— TL;DR

Hawk Translate has no servers, no analytics, and no developer-side data collection.

Your selected text is sent only to the translation provider you chose (Google, MyMemory, DeepL, Microsoft Azure, or LibreTranslate). Any API keys you enter are stored locally in your own browser and never leave it except to talk to that specific provider. We — the developers — do not see, collect, or store anything you do.

1. Who we are

Hawk Translate is an independent, open-source browser extension for Firefox, Chrome, and Edge, licensed under the MIT License. It is developed and maintained by an independent developer and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Google, Microsoft, DeepL, MyMemory, or any other translation provider.

This privacy policy explains what data the extension handles, where it goes, and what your rights are. It applies to all versions of Hawk Translate distributed through official browser add-on stores.

2. What data the extension processes

Hawk Translate processes the following data only on your device and only to perform the action you asked for:

  • Selected text and typed text. When you highlight text or type into the popup, the text is sent to the translation provider you chose so it can return a translation. It is not stored by the extension.
  • Source and target languages. The language pair you choose is sent with the translation request.
  • Your settings. Theme, bubble style, auto-close timing, default backend, and similar preferences are stored locally in your browser using the standard browser extension storage APIs.
  • API keys you enter. If you choose to use DeepL, Microsoft Azure Translator, or a self-hosted LibreTranslate endpoint, the API key or URL you enter is stored locally in your browser.

3. What we — the developer — do not do

We want to be specific, because too many "privacy policies" hide behind vague phrasing. Hawk Translate does not:

  • Operate any server that receives data from the extension.
  • Send analytics, telemetry, crash reports, or usage statistics to the developer or any third party.
  • Track your browsing history, the websites you visit, or your activity on those websites.
  • Build a profile of you, your interests, or your reading habits.
  • Sell, rent, share, or otherwise transfer your data to anyone.
  • Use cookies, fingerprinting, or any other identification mechanism on your device beyond the browser's standard extension storage for your settings.
  • Read or transmit page content automatically. The extension only acts on text you explicitly select or type.

4. Where your translated text goes

When you ask for a translation, your selected or typed text is sent directly from your browser to the translation provider you chose in Options. We do not see, log, or proxy this request. Each provider has its own privacy policy, terms of service, and data-handling practices, which apply to that request:

Google
Used by default. Subject to Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy
MyMemory
Free public translation memory. Subject to MyMemory's privacy notice: mymemory.translated.net/doc/privacy.php
DeepL
Used only if you provide your own DeepL API key. Subject to DeepL's privacy policy: deepl.com/privacy
Microsoft Azure Translator
Used only if you provide your own Azure key. Subject to Microsoft's privacy statement: privacy.microsoft.com/privacystatement
LibreTranslate
Open-source. Used only if you configure your own endpoint URL — privacy depends entirely on the instance you choose to use.

The extension does not add any identifying information to these requests beyond what the provider requires (for example, your API key for DeepL or Azure). If you change providers, requests stop going to the previous one.

5. Pronunciation and natural-voice playback

The 🔊 speaker feature uses your browser's built-in speech-synthesis capabilities (the standard Web Speech API). Depending on your operating system and browser, voice generation may happen entirely on your device or may rely on a system-level cloud voice service — that behavior is controlled by your browser/OS, not by Hawk Translate.

6. Where data is stored on your device

All settings and any API keys you enter are stored using your browser's extension storage — typically browser.storage.local or chrome.storage.local. This data:

  • Stays on your device.
  • Is removed if you uninstall the extension.
  • Can be cleared at any time from the extension's Options page.
  • Is never synced to the developer.

If you have browser-level extension sync enabled (for example, Firefox Sync or Chrome Sync), some settings may be synced between your own devices through your browser vendor's account — that sync is handled by the browser, not by Hawk Translate, and is subject to the privacy policy of your browser vendor.

7. Permissions the extension requests, and why

Hawk Translate requests only the permissions it needs to do its job:

  • Active tab / host permissions. So the extension can read your selected text on the page you're currently viewing and show the translation bubble next to it.
  • Storage. So your settings and any API keys are remembered between sessions.
  • Context menu. So the right-click "Translate" entry can appear.
  • Network requests to translation providers. So translations can be fetched from the backend you chose.

The extension does not request permissions that would let it read your history, downloads, bookmarks, or other browser data.

8. Children's privacy

Hawk Translate does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children. Because the extension does not have an account system and does not transmit data to the developer, there is no developer-side profile of any user, regardless of age. Use of third-party translation providers is subject to those providers' own age and consent terms.

9. International users

Because Hawk Translate has no server, no developer-side data collection, and no cross-border transfer of data initiated by the developer, GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and similar regulations have very limited applicability to the extension itself. When you use a third-party translation backend, that provider becomes the data processor for the text you submit, and their own jurisdiction and policies apply.

10. Open source & verifiability

Hawk Translate is licensed under the MIT License. You — or anyone — can inspect the source code to verify exactly what the extension does and does not do with your data. We strongly encourage independent review: that is the strongest privacy guarantee any extension can offer.

11. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. Material changes — for example, adding a new translation backend or any change to what the extension transmits — will be highlighted in the extension's release notes on the Firefox, Chrome, and Edge add-on stores.

12. Contact

Questions, concerns, or bug reports?

For privacy questions or to report an issue, please open an issue on the project's GitHub repository, or reach out via the contact link on the extension's listing page in your browser's add-on store.

Because there is no account system, we cannot identify "your data" on our side — there is none — but we are always happy to clarify how the extension works.
